Plasma Membrance or Cell Menbrance

  • The outermost covering layer of cell is called plasma membrance.

  • Plasma membrance is made up of lipids and proteins.

    • Functions of plasma membrance:-

  • It protect our cell from external environment.

  • It is also called selectively permeable membrance because it allow entry and exit of materials.

  • Allow diffusion of substance like carbon dioxide and oxygen.

Active and Passive Transport

Passive Transport:-When a substance moves from higher concentration to lower concentration is called passive transport.

Active Transport:-When a substance moves from lower concentration to higher concentration is called active transport.

DIFFSION

The process of movement of substances (like co2,oxygen,water etc..) lower concentration to
higher concentraton or(across plasma membrance) is called diffusion.

OSMOSIS

The process of movement of water molecules from high concentration to lower concentration across plasma membrance is called osmosis.

There are two types of osmosis:

Endosmosis:-The process in which water molecules enter into the cell is called endosmosis.
Cell swells up in this case due to more water.

Exosmosis:-The process in which the water molecules moves out from cell is called exosmosis.
Cell shrink up in this case.

Difference between Diffusion and Osmosis
Diffusion Osmosis
It occurs in all medium. It occurs only in liquid medium.
Semipermeable membrance is not required. Semipermeable membrance is required.
It is the process of moving paricles from a region of higher concentration to lower concentration. It is the process of movement of wate molecules from region of lower concentration to higher concentration across plasma membrance.
Diffusion is the process that cannot stop. Osmosis is the process that can be stopped
There are three types of solutions:-
  • Hypotonic solution:If the medium around the cell has the highest water concentration than the cell, this means that solution is very dilute and cell will gain water through the process of osmosis. Such a solution is called hypotonic solution.
  • Isotonic solution: If the medium around the cell has the equal water concentration to the cell, this means there is no movenent of water. Such a solution is called Isotonic solution.
  • Hypertonic solution: If the solution around the cell has lowest water concentration than than the cell, this means the cell lose water through osmosis(Exosmosis). Such a solution is called Hypertonic solution.

Endocytosis and Exocytosis

Endocytosis: Endocytosis is the process of capturing a substance or particle from outside the cell by engulfing it with the cell membrane, and bringing it into the cell.

example:- Amoeba obtains its food by the process of endocytosis.

Exocytosis: Exocytosis is the process of releasing a substance or paricles from the cell with the help of plasma membrance.

Nucleus

In the cell, there is a large spherical shape cell floating in the centre of cell called nucleus.

It is surrounded by cytoplasm.
It has a double layer membrance called nuclear membrance.

Functions of Nucleus:-

It regulates the cell cycle.
The nucleus is made up of several components: nuclear envelope, nuclear lamina, nucleolus, chromosomes, nucleoplasm
Nuclear membrances contain pores for materials exchange between nucleus and cytoplasm.
Nucleus contains chromosomes and DNA(deoxyribo nucleic acid)
DNA(Deoxyribo nucleic acid) molecules contains the information essential for construcing & organizing cell.
The nucleous plays an important role in cellular reproduction.

Chromosomes

Chromosomes are the genetic material present in the nucleus. It contains DNA with associated proteins.
They are present as threads called chromatin. During the growth phase of the cell, the chromatin condenses
into a much thicker structure called a chromosome. Human cells contain 23 pairs of chromosomes (46).

Cytoplasm

The cytoplasm is the fluid found inside the cell. It gives the structure to the cell and houses different organelles of the cell.The inner granular mass of the cytoplasm is often called endoplasm, the the outer,clearer(glassy) layer is called cell cortex or ectoplasm.

DNA

The chemical substance in the cells of an animal or a plant that controls what characteristics that animal or plant is called DNA.It is mainly present in nucleus.DNA stores all functions necessary to cell for functioning.Fuctional segments of DNA are calle Genes.

Chromatin

Chromatin is a thread-like structure which serves as the genetic material present inside the nucleus of the cell. It is made up of DNA and protein molecules DNA contains the hereditary information needed for the structure and function of the organism.

Cell Division

Cell division is the process through a parent cell divides into new cell to formed in new organisms.

New cell formed for growth,cell,replacement and reproduction.

There are two types of cell division:

  1. Mitosis: Miotsis is a process where a single cell divides into two daughter cell containing the original amount of genetic as parent cell.
  2. Meiosis: Meiosis is a process where a single cell divides twice to produce four cells containing half the original amount of genetic information.
